8. Fire Rate Analysis

Fire Rates Across Level Phases

Enemy fire rates do not change based on level phase. The phase system only changes which enemies spawn, not how fast those enemy types fire. Fire rate scaling only occurs via:

  • Boss phase transitions (explicit fire rate values per phase, hardcoded per boss)
  • Difficulty multiplier (global 0.5× / 0.75× / 1.0×)
  • The Void Soldier variant spawn (intrinsic to that 25% of spawns)

9. Balance Analysis & Recommendations

Current state assessment: Easy is completable but challenging. Medium is very difficult. Hard is near-impossible to complete a level due to combined bullet density and damage. The core issue is a compounding difficulty curve — enemies become denser in later phases AND each enemy type fires faster than its predecessor, with no compensating player upgrades or breathing room built into spawn timers.

1 Restructure the Difficulty Multipliers

Problem: The gap between Easy and Medium is too small in terms of survivability. Easy halves damage and fire rate. Medium is only 25% slower than Hard. The experience jumps from manageable to brutal between Medium and Hard.

Fix:

ParameterEasy (current)Easy (suggested)Medium (current)Medium (suggested)Hard (unchanged)
Damage multiplier0.5×0.4×0.75×0.6×1.0×
Fire rate multiplier0.5×0.45×0.75×0.62×1.0×
Spawn multiplier0.7×0.6×0.85×0.75×1.0×
HP regen delay30f20f60f40f120f

The key change is pulling Easy and Medium further apart from Hard, so Medium feels challenging but finishable, and Hard feels achievable by skilled players rather than impossible.

2 Cap the Void Dreadnought Fire Rate

Problem: 490ms is faster than most bosses. Two Dreadnoughts simultaneously produce 4 bullets per second combined from their cone patterns, saturating the player's dodging space alongside regular enemies.

Fix: Increase Dreadnought base fire rate to 800ms. Easy becomes 1600ms, Hard stays at 800ms. Adjust Void Dreadnought to 700ms base. This is still fast but no longer boss-tier fast for a regular enemy.

3 Cap the Void Protector Shield Value

Problem: A 2000 HP shield applied to a Void Dreadnought (1285 HP) means the combined target has 3285 effective HP — more than the first three bosses. This is unintentionally punishing in Level 4's final phase where both unit types spawn together.

Fix: Reduce the Protector shield grant to 400–600 HP. This is still meaningful protection without tripling the effective HP of high-value targets. Alternatively, exclude Dreadnoughts from being valid shield targets.

4 Add a Spawn Cap Per Enemy Type

Problem: There is no limit on how many of a given enemy type can be on screen simultaneously. In later phases of Levels 4–5, it's possible to have 4+ Void Turrets active (each cycling through 3 bullet patterns + aimed shots) alongside multiple Dreadnoughts and Snipers.

Fix: Implement a per-type cap. Suggested caps: Void Dreadnought max 1 at a time; Void Turret max 2; Void Protector max 1; Void Master max 2. This prevents the accidental stacking of high-output enemies into unplayable density.

5 Give Medium and Easy Players Extra Starting Powerups

Problem: Powerup drops are the same across all difficulties, but the player needs more firepower on harder difficulties to kill enemies before they fire. The current system doesn't help players who struggle to get early powerups.

Fix: Give Easy players 1 free firepower and 1 tracking gun at level start. Give Medium players 1 free firepower. This levels the floor of player capability without affecting the ceiling.

6 Add a Bullet Density Safety Valve

Problem: There is no mechanism that reduces bullet density when the screen becomes overloaded. The game has no concept of "too many bullets" — it can produce situations where gaps to dodge through literally do not exist.

Fix: Implement a global active-bullet counter. If more than a threshold number of bullets are simultaneously active on screen (e.g. 60 for Easy, 90 for Medium, unlimited for Hard), temporarily suppress new enemy fire until the count drops below 80%. This cap prevents mathematically impossible scenarios while preserving the intended difficulty feel.

7 Scale Enemy HP by Difficulty

Problem: Enemy HP is identical on Easy, Medium and Hard. This means on Easy, enemies live just as long as on Hard, but the player takes less damage. Easy players still have to shoot through the same HP pools while dodging — the reduced incoming damage doesn't help with time-under-fire.

Fix: Apply HP multipliers: Easy 0.7× enemy HP, Medium 0.85× enemy HP, Hard 1.0×. Bosses could have a slightly smaller adjustment: Easy 0.75×, Medium 0.9×. This meaningfully reduces time-under-fire on lower difficulties.

8 Introduce Breathing Room Between Spawn Phases

Problem: Phase transitions (e.g. Level 5 moving from Phase 3 to Phase 4) happen continuously with no pause. When phase 4 introduces Asteroid Layers alongside the existing Void and Defender mix, there's no grace period.

Fix: At each phase transition, insert a 3–5 second spawn pause (no new enemies) and briefly show a phase indicator to the player. This creates natural breathing points and helps players anticipate the upcoming challenge type.

9 Remove the speed_up Permanent Stacking Cap Issue

Problem: speed_up is permanent and unlimited. A player who collects many speed_up powerups moves faster than the game's dodge windows were designed for, which can trivialise sections that depend on the player not being able to avoid everything.

Fix: Cap speed_up at 3 stacks maximum (total speed 5 + 6 = 11). Subsequent speed_up pickups could instead grant a temporary 5-second speed boost, or convert to score points.

10 Normalise the Void Sniper Fire Rate

Problem: The Void Sniper fires at 733ms compared to the Human Sniper at 2500ms — a 3.4× difference. The Void Sniper was clearly designed to be more aggressive, but at 733ms with multiple snipers present, aimed precision shots arrive faster than many players can react across all difficulty levels.

Fix: Increase Void Sniper fire rate to 1100ms base. This is still 2.3× faster than its human counterpart and aligns with Void Soldier standard fire rate, creating a more coherent faction-wide feel.

10. The Gatekeeper — Fix Recommendations

Issue: After Phase 3 of the Gatekeeper fight, bullet density reaches a level that is not survivable regardless of player skill, difficulty setting, powerup loadout, or movement. This is not a balance problem — it is a mathematical impossibility. The available movement space becomes smaller than the gaps between bullets. The fight cannot be completed in the current state.

Root Causes

  • The Void Corruption scale increases as HP drops, directly increasing bullet output rate — by Phase 5, Void Corruption is near maximum, producing bullet generation rates the screen cannot accommodate.
  • Multiple sub-machine parts fire independently and simultaneously. At low HP, all sub-machines are firing at maximum rate in addition to the core's own pattern. There is no coordination limit between sub-machine outputs.
  • The complex fractal and chaos patterns in later phases are procedurally generated in ways that can produce zero-gap configurations — every possible movement path is blocked simultaneously.
  • The laser cooldown (300 frames) is long but the laser itself coincides with the highest-density regular bullet phases, creating moments where the player must dodge a laser and a saturated bullet field simultaneously.
  • The black hole phase (which pulls bullets and the player) occurs during high-density phases, pushing the player into bullet streams they would otherwise dodge.

Specific Fixes

Fix A — Hard Cap Void Corruption in Phase 3+

The Void Corruption variable (currently uncapped beyond the 0–1 scale) should be clamped to a maximum of 0.55 during active combat. This prevents bullet generation from reaching its current mathematically unsolvable density. At 0.55, patterns remain intense and dramatic but leave navigable gaps. Add a line:

boss.voidCorruption = Math.min(boss.voidCorruption, 0.55);

Apply this clamp in the update loop, before bullet generation calculations.

Fix B — Sub-Machine Coordination Limit

Implement a turn-based firing system for the Gatekeeper's sub-machines. Instead of all parts firing simultaneously, they should fire in a rotating sequence. Suggested implementation: assign each sub-machine a firing slot offset (e.g. offset by 200ms each). This converts simultaneous bullet walls into staggered waves that create gaps. The total damage output remains the same but is temporally distributed.

Fix C — Phase 4+ Bullet Count Cap Per Pattern

The fractal and chaos patterns should have a hard maximum bullet count per fire event — regardless of how high the corruption or chaos values are. Suggested caps:

Pattern TypeCurrent MaxSuggested Cap
Circle burstUncapped16 bullets max
Fractal spreadUncapped12 bullets max per arm
Chaos burstUncapped20 bullets per event
Void bullet cloudUncapped10 bullets max per cloud

Fix D — Disable Black Hole During High Bullet Density

The black hole mechanic (which pulls the player into existing bullet streams) should be deactivated when more than 40 active enemy bullets are currently on screen. At that density, the black hole push is a near-certain death. The black hole is a great mechanic at lower densities — it just shouldn't activate into an already-saturated screen.

Fix E — Add a Phase 5 Laser Ceasefire Window

When the Gatekeeper fires its laser in Phase 5+, all other bullet-generating sub-machines should pause for the duration of the laser beam + 60 frames after. This makes the laser feel like a distinct danger moment (which it should be) rather than an additional hazard stacked on top of already impossible bullet density.

Fix F — Introduce a Desperation Drop at Phase 5

When the Gatekeeper transitions into Phase 5 (at approximately 25% HP), drop 2–3 powerups onto the field — shield, health, and rapid fire. This gives the player a resource to collect before the most intense phase, rewards them for surviving to that point, and functionally gives them a fighting chance. At 25% HP remaining, the fight should feel like a tense final push, not a death sentence.

Fix G — Reduce Phase 5 Base Fire Rate

The Gatekeeper in Phase 5 currently fires at maximum rate across all systems simultaneously. Introduce a Phase 5-specific fire rate multiplier that reduces all Gatekeeper firing to 0.65× of the Phase 4 rate. This is counterintuitive (the boss slowing slightly as it nears death) but frames it as the boss being damaged and malfunctioning — which fits the game's Void Machine aesthetic — and makes the ending survivable.

Example: if Phase 4 fires at 300ms intervals, Phase 5 fires at ~460ms. The visual intensity can remain high through effects, screen shake, and audio — the player should feel under pressure without the firing rate being literally impossible.

Target experience for the Gatekeeper fix: Phase 3 should feel intense but survivable with good play. Phase 4 should require the player to use all their powerups and move constantly. Phase 5 should feel like a dramatic final push where survival is earned, not impossible. The boss should be the hardest thing in the game — not unbeatable.
